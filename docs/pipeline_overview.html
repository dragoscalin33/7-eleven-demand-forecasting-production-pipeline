Why Temporal Cross-Validation?

In time series, you can’t randomly shuffle data — that would let the model “peek” at the future. Our cross-validation creates 3 folds where each fold trains on the past and validates on a future 90-day window, with a 90-day gap between them (matching the actual forecast horizon).

This simulates what would happen in production: you train with historical data and predict 90 days forward. If the model performs well across all 3 folds, you can trust it will generalise to truly unseen future data.

Analogy: Imagine predicting next quarter’s weather. Random CV = mixing January and July data in training and testing (unrealistic). Temporal CV = always training on older months and testing on newer ones, exactly like you’d do in real life.
